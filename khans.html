Kerey Khan

He was one of the first founders of the Kazakh Khanate together with Zhanibek Khan. Strengthened the position of the new state.

Zhanibek Khan

Co-ruler of Korea, participated in the unification of the Kazakh lands. His reign was characterized by the strengthening of the internal structure of the khanate.

Burundik Khan

The successor of the case of Kerey and Zhanibek. He sought to preserve the independence of the Kazakh Khanate from neighboring states.

Kasym khan

Under him, the Kazakh Khanate reached its heyday. The author of the first set of Kazakh laws is "Kasym khanna kaska zholy".

Mamash Khan

A short period of government, known for political instability.

Tahir Khan

He tried to strengthen the khanate in the face of external pressure and internal contradictions.

Haknazar Khan

A long and successful reign. He restored the unity of the Kazakh lands and expanded the territory of the khanate.

Shigai Khan

He strengthened the defense capability of the khanate, negotiated with neighboring states.

Tauekel Khan

He waged active wars for independence, participated in battles with the Bukhara Khanate.

Yesim Khan

The creator of the code of laws "Yesim hannin yeski zholy". He stabilized the internal situation in the khanate.

Other Khans of the Kazakh Khanate

Name Years of Reign Description
Zhangir Khan (Salkam Zhangir) 1628–1652 Defender of the Kazakh lands from the Dzungars. He led the army in the battle of Orbulak (1643), where the Kazakhs won.
Tauke Khan 1680–1718 An outstanding ruler, the author of the code of laws "Zhety Jargy". Under him, the Kazakh Khanate became a strong state.
Abylaikhan 1771–1781 The great Kazakh Khan, the unifier of the lands. He participated in the wars against the Dzungars, strengthened diplomatic relations with Russia and China.
Kenesary Khan 1841–1847 The last Kazakh khan. He led the national liberation movement against the colonial policy of the Russian Empire.
