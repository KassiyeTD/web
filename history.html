Key Facts

Founded in 1465, the Kazakh Khanate became a beacon of unity in Central Asia.

Zheti Zhargy was a legal system introduced under Tauke Khan to maintain order.

Under Kasym Khan, the Khanate expanded its territory and influence.

The Orbulak Battle was a pivotal moment where the Khanate defended its lands.

The Kazakh Khanate thrived through trade along the Great Silk Road.

Unity of the Kazakh Zhuzes was crucial in the victory at the Battle of Anyrakay.

Khan Kerei and Zhanibek Khan laid the foundation of the Kazakh Khanate in 1465.

The Battle of Bulanty marked a significant turning point against the Dzungars.

The Khanate's territory covered vast parts of Central Asia, uniting nomadic tribes.

Legends and oral traditions played a key role in preserving the Kazakh heritage.
